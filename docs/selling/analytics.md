# Sales Analytics

Understanding your analytics is key to growing your vendor business. This guide covers the metrics, reports, and insights available to vendors.

## Analytics Dashboard

### Accessing Analytics

1. Go to **Commerce** in navigation
2. Click **Analytics** or **Dashboard**
3. View your performance data

### Dashboard Overview

At-a-glance metrics:
- **Revenue** — Today, week, month, all-time
- **Sales** — Transaction count
- **Products** — Active product count
- **Customers** — Unique buyers

## Key Metrics

### Revenue Metrics

**Gross Revenue**
- Total sales before commission
- Raw revenue generated

**Net Revenue**
- Your earnings after commission
- What you actually receive

**Average Order Value**
- Revenue per transaction
- Indicates pricing effectiveness

**Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**
- Subscription revenue
- For subscription products

### Sales Metrics

**Total Sales**
- Number of transactions
- Count of orders

**Conversion Rate**
- Sales / Visitors
- Percentage who buy

**Refund Rate**
- Refunds / Sales
- Quality indicator

### Product Metrics

**Best Sellers**
- Highest revenue products
- Most popular offerings

**Views**
- Product page visits
- Discovery measure

**View-to-Purchase Rate**
- How many viewers buy
- Product page effectiveness

### Customer Metrics

**New Customers**
- First-time buyers
- Audience growth

**Repeat Customers**
- Returning purchasers
- Loyalty indicator

**Customer Lifetime Value**
- Total revenue per customer
- Long-term value

## Understanding Your Data

### Interpreting Revenue Trends

**Rising Revenue**
- Growing audience
- Effective marketing
- Product-market fit

**Flat Revenue**
- Stable business
- May need new products
- Consider expansion

**Declining Revenue**
- Competition increase
- Product aging
- Market changes

### Interpreting Conversion

**High Conversion (>5%)**
- Strong product-market fit
- Effective descriptions
- Good pricing

**Low Conversion (under 1%)**
- Targeting issues
- Pricing problems
- Description improvements needed

### Interpreting Refunds

**Low Refunds (under 2%)**
- Quality product
- Clear expectations
- Happy customers

**High Refunds (>5%)**
- Quality issues
- Mismatched expectations
- Consider improvements

## Growth Strategies

### Based on Analytics

**If views are low:**
- Improve SEO/discovery
- Market externally
- Optimize listing title

**If conversion is low:**
- Improve descriptions
- Add social proof
- Adjust pricing
- Better preview

**If refunds are high:**
- Improve product quality
- Set clearer expectations
- Better targeting

### Optimization Loop

1. Check analytics regularly
2. Identify issues/opportunities
3. Make targeted changes
4. Measure results
5. Iterate

## Comparative Benchmarks

### Typical Ranges

| Refund Rate | Over 5% | 2-5% | Under 2% |
| Repeat Rate | Under 10% | 20-30% | Over 40% |
